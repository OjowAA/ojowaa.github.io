A Electron
The electron is a subatomic particle whose electric charge is negative one elementary charge. Electrons belong to the first generation of the lepton particle family, and are generally thought to be elementary particles because they have no known components or substructure.

A Proton
A proton is a stable subatomic particle, symbol p , p⁺ , H⁺, or ¹H⁺ with a positive electric charge of +1e elementary charge. Its mass is slightly less than that of a neutron and 1836 times the mass of an electron.

An Atom
An atom is the smallest unit of ordinary matter that forms a chemical element. Every solid, liquid, gas, and plasma is composed of neutral or ionized atoms. Atoms are extremely small, typically around 100 picometers across.

A Molecule
A molecule is a group of two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds; depending on context, the term may or may not include ions which satisfy this criterion.
